With Apple's next major iOS release on the horizon, INR Mate development is shifting focus for a short while.

Rather than adding more new features immediately, we are taking time to make sure INR Mate is ready for iOS 27 and can take advantage of the features that genuinely improve the app for people tracking INR results and warfarin doses.

This work includes iOS 27 compatibility, deeper integration with Apple's latest developer tools, and continued exploration of ways to make INR logging faster and easier across iPhone, iPad, Apple Watch, Siri and Shortcuts.

It does not mean INR Mate is standing still. The work is simply moving behind the curtain for a little while, where the foundations live.

Once the iOS 27 work is ready, we will share more about what is changing and which new features will be coming to INR Mate.

Thank you to everyone who has downloaded INR Mate, subscribed, shared feedback, or helped shape the app so far.
